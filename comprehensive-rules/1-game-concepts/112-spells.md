# 112. Spells

Chapter: 1. Game Concepts
Source: Magic: The Gathering Comprehensive Rules (effective June 19, 2026)

## Rules

<a id="rule-112-1"></a>
112.1. A spell is a card on the stack. As the first step of being cast (see rule [601](../6-spells-abilities-and-effects/601-casting-spells.md#rule-601), “Casting Spells”), the card becomes a spell and is moved to the top of the stack from the zone it was in, which is usually its owner’s hand. (See rule [405](../4-zones/405-stack.md#rule-405), “Stack.”) A spell remains on the stack as a spell until it resolves (see rule [608](../6-spells-abilities-and-effects/608-resolving-spells-and-abilities.md#rule-608), “Resolving Spells and Abilities”), is countered (see rule [701.6](../7-additional-rules/701-keyword-actions.md#rule-701-6)), or otherwise leaves the stack. For more information, see section [6](../6-spells-abilities-and-effects/README.md), “Spells, Abilities, and Effects.”

<a id="rule-112-1a"></a>
112.1a A copy of a spell is also a spell, even if it has no card associated with it. See rule [707.10](../7-additional-rules/707-copying-objects.md#rule-707-10).

<a id="rule-112-1b"></a>
112.1b Some effects allow a player to cast a copy of a card; if the player does, that copy is a spell as well. See rule [707.12](../7-additional-rules/707-copying-objects.md#rule-707-12).

<a id="rule-112-2"></a>
112.2. A spell’s owner is the same as the owner of the card that represents it, unless it’s a copy. In that case, the owner of the spell is the player under whose control it was put on the stack. A spell’s controller is, by default, the player who put it on the stack. Every spell has a controller.

<a id="rule-112-2a"></a>
112.2a Some effects instruct a player to create a copy of a card and say they may cast it. In that case, the owner of that copy is the player who is instructed to create it and given permission to cast it.

<a id="rule-112-3"></a>
112.3. A noncopy spell’s characteristics are the same as those printed on its card, as modified by any continuous effects. See rule [613](../6-spells-abilities-and-effects/613-interaction-of-continuous-effects.md#rule-613), “Interaction of Continuous Effects.”

<a id="rule-112-4"></a>
112.4. If an effect of a resolving spell or ability changes any characteristics of a permanent spell, the effect continues to apply to the permanent when the spell resolves. See rule [400.7](../4-zones/400-general.md#rule-400-7).
Example: If an effect changes a black creature spell to white, the creature is white when it enters the battlefield and remains white for the duration of the effect changing its color.
