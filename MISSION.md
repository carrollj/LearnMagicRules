## Why

I want to deepen an already solid understanding of Magic: The Gathering rules so I can reason through obscure interactions without relying on intuition or ad hoc rulings.

## Success looks like

- I can explain the layer system, including timestamps and dependency, with confidence.
- I can determine when triggered abilities trigger, when they wait, and when they are actually put on the stack.
- I can resolve tricky timing and ordering questions around casting spells, activating abilities, paying costs, and crewing Vehicles.
- I can cite the relevant Comprehensive Rules sections for the interactions I study.

## Constraints

- Prefer authoritative sources first, especially the current Comprehensive Rules.
- Focus on advanced rules topics rather than beginner gameplay fundamentals.
- Use concrete examples and edge cases to test understanding.

## Out of scope

- Introductory explanations of turn structure, phases, the stack, or basic priority.
- Card strategy, deckbuilding, or metagame discussion.
- Tournament policy unless it becomes necessary to distinguish policy from rules.
